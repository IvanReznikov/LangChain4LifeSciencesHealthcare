Conclusions

1. Clear Regression in Survival Rates with Increased Comorbidity: As the combined risk score increases, there is a stark decline in actual 10-year survival percentages.

2. Predicted vs. Actual Survival: The predicted survival percentages closely align with actual survival rates for lower risk scores up to 3.

3. Implications for Patient Management: These findings imply that patients with higher comorbidity scores present higher risks for mortality.
